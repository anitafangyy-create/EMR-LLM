
【Figure Legends】

Figure 1. Missing Data Landscape in Pancreatic Cancer EMR
(a) Heatmap of missing values for 100 representative patients across 16 key 
    clinical features. Red indicates missing data.
(b) Heatmap of the 30 most missing features across 100 patients with highest 
    missing rates, showing co-occurrence patterns.
(c) Patient clustering by missing count, revealing five distinct missing 
    pattern subgroups (indicated by blue boundary lines).
(d) Feature co-occurrence matrix showing which clinical variables tend to 
    be missing together (darker red = higher co-occurrence).

Figure 2. Hierarchical Imputation Framework Architecture
Schematic of the three-tier imputation system: Tier 1 (rule-based deterministic 
logic), Tier 2 (LLM lightweight inference), and Tier 3 (LLM deep reasoning with 
RAG). Arrow thickness indicates data flow volume; colors represent confidence 
levels (green = high, yellow = medium, red = low).

Figure 3. Imputation Quality Validation
(a) Distribution of LLM confidence scores for clinical stage inference, 
    stratified by availability of supporting evidence.
(b) Concordance between LLM-inferred stages and available pathological stages.
(c) Survival model performance (C-index) across imputation tiers.
(d) Calibration curves for 12-month survival probability predictions.

Figure 4. External Validation Against Published Literature
Forest plot comparing key epidemiological parameters (age, gender ratio, 
diabetes prevalence, jaundice rate, median survival) between our cohort 
(post-imputation) and reference studies (SEER, NCDB, MIMIC-IV).
